### License Plate Rules
```
IMPORTANT: License plates must NEVER be fully legible or readable in any shot.
The plates are Slovak EU-format:
- Motorcycle: BT-KWSH2 (square 2-line format)
- Car: XX-BMWM5 (standard long EU format)

Acceptable plate visibility:
- Blurred/out of focus plate with visible blue EU strip and "SK" marking
- Partial view showing only the EU blue band with stars and SK letters
- Angled view where plate is partially obscured or motion-blurred
- Plate hidden by camera angle, shadows, or cropping
- Distant shot where plate is too small to read but EU blue strip is recognizable

NEVER generate readable text on the plate. If plate is in frame, it must be
blurred, partially obscured, or at an angle that prevents reading.
```

## License Plate Handling Tips

1. **Best approach**: Frame shots to naturally exclude plates (front 3/4, cockpit POV, detail shots)
2. **If plate in frame**: Add to prompt: `license plate heavily blurred, unreadable, only blue EU strip with SK visible`
3. **Motion shots**: Motion blur naturally obscures plates - use rolling/panning shot descriptions
4. **Night shots**: Shadows and lighting naturally hide plate details
5. **Negative prompt**: Always include `(readable text on license plate:1.5)` to prevent generated text
6. **Post-processing**: If plate appears readable, use inpainting to blur it out while keeping EU blue band with SK
7. **Distance shots**: Plates become naturally unreadable at distance, blue EU strip remains as color accent
